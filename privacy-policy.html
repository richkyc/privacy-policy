Preamble

RICHKYC PRIVATE LIMITED (hereinafter referred to as "Company", "we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of your personal data. This Privacy Policy describes, in compliance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDPA) and the Digital Personal Data Protection Rules, 2025, how we collect, process, store, disclose, and protect the personal data of visitors to our corporate website (RichKYC.com) and, where applicable, users of our subsidiary brand platforms.

Important: This policy applies to the corporate parent website only. Each subsidiary brand (PaaIPay, ApnaSell, HostITS, etc.) maintains its own separate, detailed privacy policy specific to the services offered on those platforms. In the event of inconsistency, the subsidiary's privacy policy shall prevail for that specific service.

1. Definitions

Term Definition
Data Principal The individual to whom the personal data relates. Includes the individual's guardian (in case of children) or lawful representative.
Data Fiduciary RICHKYC PRIVATE LIMITED, which determines the purpose and means of processing personal data.
Personal Data Any data about an individual who is identifiable by or in relation to such data.
Processing Automated or manual operation performed on personal data, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, retrieval, use, disclosure, and erasure.
Consent Manager An entity registered with the Data Protection Board to enable a Data Principal to give, manage, review, or withdraw consent.

3. Notice at Collection

We Collect When:

  • You voluntarily contact us via email, contact forms, or inquiry submissions.
  • You subscribe to our corporate newsletters or investor updates.
  • You apply for employment through our Careers portal.
  • You interact with our websites in a way that generates server logs.

We DO NOT:

  • Collect sensitive personal data such as Aadhaar, PAN, financial account details, biometric information, caste, religion, or sexual orientation on this corporate website.
  • Sell, rent, or trade your personal data to third parties.
  • Use personal data for automated decision-making or profiling without explicit consent.

6. Consent Framework

6.1 Obtaining Consent

  • Explicitly: Through checkboxes, form submissions, and specific consent requests.
  • Implicitly: By continued use of the website after being provided with clear notice.

6.2 Withdrawal of Consent

You have the absolute right to withdraw your consent at any time.

Method: Email grievance@richkyc.com with subject "WITHDRAWAL OF CONSENT".

Effect of Withdrawal: Upon withdrawal, we will cease processing your personal data for the consented purpose. Data processed prior to withdrawal remains lawful.

8. Data Security Measures

We implement the following security safeguards as required under Section 8(5) of DPDPA, 2023:

Technical Measures

  • Encryption: TLS 1.3 for data in transit; AES-256 for data at rest.
  • Access Controls: Role-based access control (RBAC), principle of least privilege.
  • Firewalls: Web Application Firewall (WAF), DDoS protection.
  • Backup: Encrypted daily backups with off-site storage.

Organizational Measures

  • Training: Annual data protection training for all employees.
  • Confidentiality: All personnel bound by confidentiality clauses.
  • Vendor Assessment: Third-party processors assessed for compliance.
  • Incident Response: Documented procedure for data breach response.

9. Data Breach Protocol

In the event of a personal data breach, the Company shall:

  • Containment: Immediately isolate affected systems.
  • Assessment: Determine scope within 24 hours.
  • Notification: Inform Data Protection Board and Data Principals as required under Rule 8 of DPDP Rules, 2025.

10. Data Principal Rights

  • Right to Access: Confirmation of processing, summary of data, and identities of processors.
  • Right to Correction: Update inaccurate, misleading, or incomplete data.
  • Right to Erasure: Right to be forgotten if consent is withdrawn or purpose is met.
  • Right to Grievance Redressal: Right to have complaints addressed promptly.
  • Right to Nominate: Nominate an individual to exercise rights in event of incapacity.

11. Children's Data

We do not knowingly collect or process personal data of individuals below the age of 18 years on this corporate website. If you believe we have inadvertently collected data of a child, please contact our Grievance Officer immediately for erasure.

12. Cross-Border Data Transfer

Personal data is primarily processed and stored within the geographical territory of India. We do not transfer personal data outside India unless explicitly consented or to a jurisdiction notified by the Central Government under Section 16 of DPDPA.
